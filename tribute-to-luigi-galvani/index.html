Luigi Galvani

The pioneer of Bioelectricity

Oil Paint Portrait of Luigi Galvani, Italian physicist, with his experiment.

Time Line of Luigi Galvani:

  • 1737 - Born in Bologna, Papal States
  • 1755 - Started attending Medicine Course at the faculty of the Arts of the University of Bologna.
  • 1759 - Graduated with degrees in medicine and philosophy.
  • 1762 - First publication: A dissertation on the structure, function, and pathology of bones. Became a permanent anatomist of the university and was appointed honorary lecturer of surgery.
  • 1767 - First publication: A dissertation on the structure, function, and pathology of bones. Became a permanent anatomist of the university and was appointed honorary lecturer of surgery.
  • 1775 - Was appointed professor and lecturer at the University of Bologna.
  • 1776 - moved from the position of lecturer of surgery to theoretical anatomy and obtained an appointment at the Academy of Sciences, University of Bologna.
  • 1780 - Beginning of extensive and meticulous series of investigations into the irritable responses elicited by static electricity in properly prepared frogs.
  • 1794 and 1797 - Annouced that convulsive contractions could be produced merely by touching nerves to muscles.
  • 1798 - Galvani died, uncertain about the future of his theory.
  • 1840 - Emil du Bois-Reymond, German physician and physiologist, successfully measured the injury potential from frog muscle, therefore proving that bioelectric forces exist within living tissue.
